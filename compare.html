Kibi vs. iExit & NexIT

iExit and NexIT are highway exit guides — they tell you what's at upcoming exits while you're already on the road. They're useful for quick lookups, but they don't plan your trip ahead of time.

What Kibi does differently: Kibi analyzes your entire route before you leave, scores every exit using Google Places data, and recommends the single best stop that combines gas, food, and a bathroom — timed to when your vehicle actually needs to refuel. No scrolling, no guessing. One recommendation, every time.

Kibi vs. Roadtrippers

Roadtrippers excels at road trip discovery — finding quirky attractions, scenic routes, and multi-day itineraries. It's built for the journey, not the logistics.

What Kibi does differently: Kibi is built for highway efficiency. When you're driving 4+ hours on the interstate and just need one great stop for gas, food, and a bathroom, Kibi finds it. It calculates when you need to stop based on your vehicle's fuel range and picks the exit that has everything in one place.

Kibi vs. GasBuddy

GasBuddy is the go-to for finding cheap gas prices. It's great at one thing: saving money on fuel.

What Kibi does differently: Kibi isn't just about gas — it finds highway exits where you can get gas, food, AND a bathroom together, scored by quality and convenience. It plans your stops around your vehicle's range so you never pull off at a bad exit out of desperation. Gas is part of the equation, but Kibi optimizes the whole pitstop.

Kibi vs. Google Maps

Google Maps is the world's most popular navigation app. It routes you from A to B with incredible accuracy — but it doesn't help you find the best place to stop along the way.

What Kibi does differently: Kibi is a pitstop planning layer that works WITH Google Maps. It analyzes your route, finds the best exit for gas, food, and a bathroom, and then deep-links directly into Google Maps with all your waypoints pre-loaded. Kibi handles the planning; Google Maps handles the navigation.

Kibi vs. Waze

Waze is great for real-time traffic and community-reported road conditions. Like Google Maps, it's a navigation app — not a pitstop planner.

What Kibi does differently: Kibi plans your pitstops before you start driving and deep-links into Waze with all waypoints pre-loaded. Waze gets you there; Kibi tells you where to stop.

Kibi vs. Apple Maps

Apple Maps provides smooth, privacy-focused navigation integrated into the Apple ecosystem. It gives turn-by-turn directions but doesn't plan your road trip pitstops.

What Kibi does differently: Kibi analyzes your vehicle's fuel range, scores every highway exit on your route, and finds the one stop where you can get gas, food, and a bathroom. It then launches Apple Maps with all your waypoints ready to go. Apple Maps navigates; Kibi plans the stops.
